# Research Workflow

## 1. Thesis First

Before deep collection, write:

- one-sentence thesis
- target reader
- core question
- companies or actors in scope
- out-of-scope claims
- minimum evidence standard
- final article structure

If the task is drifting into system design, stop and restate the article objective.

## 2. Source Collection

Classify every source at intake:

- official self-narrative: website, blog, product docs, model card, policy statement
- podcast/interview: founder, product lead, researcher, investor, journalist
- media translation: news, longform analysis, Chinese/English reposts
- market/capital: VC report, app intelligence report, financial filing, pricing page
- developer/community: GitHub, Hugging Face, OpenRouter, Hacker News, Reddit, X
- counter-narrative: lawsuit, failed adoption, criticism, security issue, cost challenge

Record failures and access limitations, but keep them out of the final body unless the limitation is itself analytically important.

## 3. Company Analysis Card

Create one card per company:

1. Narrative history: how the company's external story changed over time.
2. Narrative position: what default language or workflow the company tries to own.
3. Construction mechanism: product, API, docs, keynote, blog, pricing, ads, podcast.
4. Influence pathway: market, developers, media, practitioners, capital, society.
5. Power outcome: default vocabulary, architecture, workflow, price anchor, legitimacy frame.
6. Counter-narrative: what could invalidate or narrow the company's story.

Do not average word count across companies. Let importance, evidence, and complexity determine length.

## 4. Podcast And Interview Processing

Do not merely summarize episodes. Extract:

- framing terms
- company comparison logic
- examples that translate company language into action
- audience addressed by the channel
- whether the speaker supports, criticizes, or reinterprets the narrative
- how the episode can be used in a company block or synthesis section

Treat transcripts, third-party summaries, and local ASR differently. Do not quote exact wording from ASR unless manually verified.

## 5. Assembly

Assemble around the thesis:

1. Core insights
2. Research scope
3. Company blocks
4. Commonality and divergence
5. Media/podcast/transmission system
6. Global or cross-market synthesis
7. Conclusion or final synthesis
8. Reference appendix

If the article becomes a list of materials, rewrite around behavior change and system impact.
